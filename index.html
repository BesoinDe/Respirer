Breathe Easier, Live Healthier: Rethink Car Use in Our City

Reducing car dependency improves public health for everyone in our community.

How Excessive Car Use Impacts Our Health:

  • Air Pollution (Exhaust): Cars release harmful gases (NOx, Ozone precursors) and fine particles (PM2.5) linked to asthma, bronchitis, reduced lung function, heart attacks, and strokes.
  • Noise Pollution: Constant traffic noise contributes to stress, high blood pressure, sleep disturbances, and cardiovascular problems.
  • Tyre & Brake Wear: Often overlooked, tiny particles from tyre and brake wear significantly pollute air and water, posing respiratory and cardiovascular risks similar to exhaust fumes.
  • Sedentary Lifestyles: Over-reliance on cars discourages walking and cycling, contributing to obesity, diabetes, and heart disease.
  • Road Accidents: Cars are a major source of injuries and fatalities, impacting physical and mental well-being in the community.
